Publishers, Distributors, Subscribers

SQL Server uses the publishing industry as a metaphor to represent the replication model. In SQL Server Replication:

  • Publishers store the master copy of important data in a database. This information is made available in the form of a publication.
  • Distributors collect information from the publications and send it to subscribers over the network.
  • Subscribers tell the distributor which publications they are interested in receiving.  When published data is received from a distributor, a subscriber stores this information in its own database.

Replication in the BHS:

  • SAC: Publishes the entire database and acts as its own distributor to send the database information to subscribers.
  • DHS: Subscribes to the publication provided by the SAC.
