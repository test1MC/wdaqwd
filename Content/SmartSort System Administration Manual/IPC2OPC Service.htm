IPC2OPC Service

The IPC2OPC service allows the Upper-Level software services to indicate their status on the HMI. The service simply acts a bridge from the Upper-Level software to the PLCs and means that individual services do not need to maintain their own connections. This service translates IPC (Inter Process Communication) from the various services listed above into OPC which is the communication medium for a computer to talk to a PLC.

Kepware OPC server is installed on the DHS and is used for the OPC side of this link. The IPC messaging medium is used throughout the upper-level software services for more than just this communication, but the heartbeats and alarm indicators have their own dedicated channel.

The IPC2OPC service needs to run at all times so that the HMI displays the updated status of the various services. The indicators that exist on the HMI Communication screen will not display the correct statuses if this service is not running correctly. Heartbeats for all services will appear to be faulted in this case.
