Monitoring Alarms and Fault Modes

Alarms occur when a system fault is detected by the PLCs. On the HMI, these alarms are shown on the Alarm Summary screen and by clicking the Alarm button (see section 7.2.1 Monitoring Alarms with the HMI). In the Web Client, these alarms are shown on the System Dashboard, as well as being accessible from some advanced searches and reports (see section 7.2.3 Fault Mode Pop-Up Window).

Alarms on the HMI can be in one of three states:

  • In Alarm – A fault has just occurred.
  • Acknowledged – An operator has acknowledged that the alarm exists, but it has not cleared yet.
  • Out of Alarm: the alarm has cleared.

Alarms have colors that correspond to their severity (see section 7.1.1 HMI Display Conventions). These colors will match the background of each alarm shown in the alarm banners. When an alarm is acknowledged, its Acknowledgement Time will be set, and the alarm text will turn black. Acknowledged or unacknowledged alarms that are cleared by the PLC (e.g. fault gets reset) will be removed from the alarm banners.
