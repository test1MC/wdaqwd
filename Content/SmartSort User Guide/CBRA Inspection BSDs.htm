CBRA Inspection (BSDs)

Baggage Status Display (BSD) stations are located at Baggage Inspection Tables (BIT) in the Checked Baggage Resolution Area (CBRA). BSDs are used to assist users in the inspection of bags that are not cleared automatically by security screening. Bags will also be sent to the CBRA area if they are too large to be processed using the Inline Screening Devices (ISDs) or if they are lost in tracking between being processed by the ISDs and being diverted to the Cleared portion of the sortation system.

In general terms, the CBRA Inspection Process is described below:

  1. A bag is sent to the CBRA area.
  1. The bag stops at a Baggage Removal Point (BRP).
  • In contingency operations, bags will stop at the first BRP station and a bag tag can be printed for each bag.
  1. The bag's information is displayed on the BSD at the associated BIT terminal.
  1. The agent uses the BSD application to view the associated bag data and possible actions for the bag.
  1. The agent follows SOP for reconciling the bag by reinserting it into the BHS, sending it back to be scanned by the ISD machine, or dealing with it on the table.
