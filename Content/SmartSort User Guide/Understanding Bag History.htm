Understanding Bag History

At each critical stage throughout the life of a bag in the Baggage Handling System, the system records transactions for easy tracking of what happened to the bag. If the bag is associated with an IATA barcode, the location of the event will be recorded in that barcode's Last Seen Location. The following transactions are recorded:

  • BSM Message Received (also BSM Change and Delete)
  • Bag Measured at Dimensioner
  • Bag Scanned at CBIS ATR
  • Pre-ISD Message (Bag hand off to ISD Machine)
  • Screening Update (Bag Security Status)
  • Post-ISD Message (Bag hand off from ISD Machine)
  • CBRA Arrival (Reason for sending to CBRA)
  • Bag Released at MEC
  • Bag Routed or Sorted (Diverted or Pushed) to its destination or a new conveyor line.
  • Bag Failed to Sort
  • Bag Status Update (e.g. bag is lost in tracking, or unknown bag is detected)

These transactions are relevant for system research (generating reports, performing searches, viewing statistics, viewing alarms, etc.). For details, refer to sections 9 Understanding Web Client Reports, 10 Understanding Search Results, 7.2 Monitoring Alarms and Fault Modes, and 7.4 Monitoring Live Statistics.
